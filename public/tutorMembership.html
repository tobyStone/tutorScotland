‚ú® Why CPD Matters

At Tutors Alliance Scotland, we believe that continuous professional development (CPD) is essential for maintaining high standards of tuition, safeguarding learners, and staying up to date with best practice. All TAS members are expected to commit to regular training as part of their professional role.

‚úÖ Core Training Required for Membership

To become a TAS-accredited tutor, you must complete the following mandatory training before or during your application process:

1. Safeguarding Training (Basic/Level 1)

Required: All tutors must complete basic safeguarding training, refreshed every 2 years.

Options:

  • Provide evidence of recent training completed elsewhere (e.g., NSPCC, EduCare, Virtual College)
  • Or complete our recommended online course [insert link if available]

2. Data Protection & GDPR Training

Required: All members must complete GDPR and data protection training to ensure compliance when handling pupil and parent information.

  • Submit a certificate from a recent course

üë©‚Äçüè´ Tutor FAQs (For prospective and current members)

1. Who can become a member of Tutors Alliance Scotland?

Tutors Alliance Scotland is open to professional tutors who meet our membership criteria, including qualifications, experience, safeguarding checks and a commitment to our STRIVE values. Whether you're working independently or as part of an organisation, we'd love to hear from you.

2. What are the benefits of joining?

Members receive professional recognition, access to CPD and networking events, a listing in our tutor directory, resources to support best practice and the chance to be part of a growing movement to raise tutoring standards across Scotland.

3. What checks do I need to have in place?

All members must hold a current PVG (Protecting Vulnerable Groups) check and provide proof of qualifications and references. We also require tutors to complete basic safeguarding training.

4. Do I need to be based in Scotland to join?

Yes, at this time we are focused on supporting tutors who are based in or delivering tuition to pupils in Scotland. As we grow, this may evolve.

5. Is there a cost to join?

Yes, there is a membership fee to support our vetting process, resources and community activities. Full details are on our Tutor Membership page. We are a not for profit organisation so money raised from fees go toward supporting and running TAS.

6. How do I apply?

You can complete an online application via our Tutor Membership page. We'll then review your documents, complete necessary checks and be in touch. (WILL INCLUDE A LINK TO MEMBER MOJO SIGN UP PAGE)

7. What CPD opportunities do you offer?

We provide access to regular webinars, training events and recommended partner courses. Members also receive discounted rates for select events and from our incentive partners.

8. Will you refer pupils to me?

Members may be found through our tutor directory and we sometimes facilitate referrals through our social mission projects or partners. However, we are not an agency or tutoring platform.

9. Can I use the TAS logo once I'm a member?

Yes. Once approved, you'll receive our membership badge to use on your website, social media and marketing materials.

10. What happens if a concern is raised about me?

We take concerns seriously and have a clear complaints and disciplinary process to ensure transparency, fairness and safeguarding for all parties involved.
